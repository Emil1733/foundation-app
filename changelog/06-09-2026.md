## Objective: Index Consolidation, PageRank Routing, and Performance Recovery

This update addresses crawl anomalies identified in the June 9, 2026 GSC audit: duplicate indexing (WWW vs non-WWW), de-indexation of programmatic soil reports (/learn), and TTFB/prefetching crawl overhead.

*   **JSON-LD Schema Safeguards:** Added strict conditional checks in [app/learn/[slug]/page.tsx](file:///c:/Users/tevat/nicheanalyzer/foundation-app/app/learn/%5Bslug%5D/page.tsx) to ensure incomplete geological profiles do not output broken JSON schemas.

#### Benchmark 1: WWW &rarr; Non-WWW Consolidation
*   **Current Baseline:** NON-WWW is at 42% of impressions (183 vs 251 on WWW).
*   **Expectation:** The `non-www` domain should account for **>80% of total property impressions** as Google consolidates the indexing signals.

#### Benchmark 2: Programmatic `/learn` Re-indexing

*   **Expectation:** At least **5–10 additional `/learn` soil report pages** should re-appear in search results with >0 impressions, driven by the new homepage link grid and service page widgets.

*   **Expectation:** The query should recover to the **top 5 positions** as Googlebot crawls and weighs the restored exact-match title tag.

*   **Expectation:** Queries containing `"settling"` or `"settlement"` should improve by **5–10 positions** on average across all TX locations due to the title tag optimization in the articles.

*   **Expectation:** Google's reported average crawl response time (GSC Settings &rarr; Crawl Stats) should **drop below 500ms** due to client connection pooling.

#### Benchmark 6: Click Growth

*   **Expectation:** Total Property Clicks should grow to **5+ clicks** in the 30-day window as keywords transition to page 1.
